Pun Car Band

Pun Car Band

Established 15 years ago, this band has serenaded audiences all over Taiwan, including performing in many temples. The band's core mission is to cater to the needs of the elderly. They frequently perform at nursing homes and kindergartens, regaling listeners with traditional or modified Taiwanese nursery rhymes. With over 2,000 performances under their belt, this band has dedicated themselves to give back to the older generation. Perhaps during your next visit to Tainan in southern Taiwan, you'll chance upon them. Harmonizing tales of Taiwan's history within the walls of a temple, their music extends warmth to every corner of the city.

Tjakudayi

TAI Body Theatre

Tjakudayi is a dancing performance premiered by TAI Body Theatre in 2013. This work is inspired from the novel ’ How to Say I Love You’ by Ising Suaiyung, a member of the dance troupe, based on the Paiwan tribe’s Tjalja’avus community in Pingtung. It presents the traditional life of the Paiwan people, continuing the family line in the music passed down by their ancestors. Whether it’s seasonal rituals or life customs, the Paiwan people always express their feelings timely through songs. Through singing, they express their longing for each other, because only through singing can they be together.

Toughen up, off we sail

LIN Zong-fan/Feng-Chung-Teng spirit guiding group

The main theme revolves around the imagery of ancestors crossing from China to Taiwan, spanning the Black Water Ditch and taking root upon arrival. It is accompanied by the Hengchun folk song “Si Hsiang Chi” (Thoughts Arising),’ expressing the longing and separation between loved ones who embarked on a distant journey to Taiwan, hoping for a safe passage across the Black Water Ditch(Taiwan Strait). To ensure the safety of their loved ones, prayers to the deities are offered, and the assistance of a shaman is sought, chanting the ’Passing Gate’ ritual, unique to the Hongmao Villagers, to pray for the safe passage of their kin. Upon arriving safely, they settle and cultivate the land in Taiwan, leading to a peaceful and prosperous life, symbolized by the ’Garden Stroll’ funeral dirge, singing of the intentions for a fruitful and fulfilling life.

SOMETHING ABOUT US↔STRAWBERRY

DJ ELVIS LIN

In the name of Paris Olympics, this performance combines the groovy and energetic music from France and Taiwan, mixing tracks from French artists like Daft Punk, Phoenix, Françoise Hardy with Taiwanese creations such as Sticky Rice’s “Paris Strawberry”, Kay Huang’s “Blue Beer Sea”, and JADE EYES’s “By The Sea Side”. Chiu Shu-chan

Chiu Shu-chan

Shu-Chan Chiu, a singer-songwriter hailing from Meinong and Liugui in Kaohsiung, has nurtured a lifelong affinity for her mother tongue, Hakka, since childhood. Securing the top honors in the Hakka category of both the 2020 and 2022 Taiwan Music Composition and Songwriting Contest, Shu-Chan Chiu triumphed with her singles Mong and Nightmare respectively. In July 2023, she unveiled her inaugural Hakka composition album, Shape Of Life. Completely independently produced and released, the album earned her the prestigious Best Folk Song Award at the 14th Golden Indie Music Awards. The album comprises soulful ballads about life, predominantly rooted in acoustic guitar melodies. Her mellifluous voice, akin to the first rays of dawn's warmth, intertwines with the morning dew, gently guiding the listener forward with tranquil assurance.
Singer-songwriter and guitar | CHIU Shu-Chan

Aljenljeng

ABAO

Abao (Aljenljeng)
Singer, songwriter, and member of the Paiwan tribe in Taitung.

In 2003, Abao made her debut with the girl group Abao & Brandy and secured the Golden Melody Award for Best Vocal Ensemble the subsequent year. Following her award win, Abao shifted her focus to music creation and immersed herself in behind-the-scenes production. In 2012, the producer of Taiwan Indigenous TV invited her to resume her role as a host on screen. As her hosting duties brought her closer to indigenous tribal cultures across Taiwan, she became increasingly involved in learning about and promoting indigenous cultures. While hosting, in 2014 she collaborated with her mother and grandmother to record the Paiwan folk ballad album “The East Payuan Folk And Three Generations”. In 2016, she released her first native-language album, “Vavayan Woman”. Her second native-language album, “Kinakaian Mother's Tongue”, released in 2019, garnered her eight Golden Melody Award nominations. Currently, she continues to intertwine mother tongue culture with multi-field performance work and advocates for the creation of mother tongue music by a new generation of indigenous youth.

New Taiwanese Opera

Birthday Wishes: Feudal Times – The Power of the People and the Challenges of Democracy

On the empress's birthday, all the ministers come to offer their congratulations. The empress discloses that she is considering stepping down from the throne and relinquishing her authority. She is willing to heed the counsel of the ministers and proceed with enfeoffment. The ministers begin to flatter her, speaking sweetly and persuasively. The empress is delighted, and the ministers receive what they desire. However, there is one incompetent minister who exploits the people. He flatters the empress, offering goods and favors that are not rightfully his. This leads to a people's rebellion, resulting in the overthrow of his rule. The people proclaim themselves rulers, but the problems persist; the new officials promptly reverse course and start flattering their new masters.

Questioning the Divine: Hearkening to the Mortals - Part C: As You Like It

New Taiwanese Opera

Fighting Beauty Competition: Embracing the Essence of Love

The prince's companions convene a concubine selection assembly on his behalf. Although the competing girls each possess talents admired by the prince, none manage to capture his heart. During the assembly, a quarrel erupts between two of the women, Xiuying and Fengjiao. But paradoxically, the more they quarrel, the more they find themselves drawn to each other. The prince, embracing the unexpected turn of events, decides to accept both Xiuying and Fengjiao. It transpires that Fengjiao had actually entered the concubine selection competition on behalf of her brother. The prince accepts her entering the contest. Only then do the friends realize that they have misunderstood the prince's liking from beginning to end.

New Taiwanese Opera

Volunteer Fighters and the Military: Tolerance and Virtue

Refugee Ah-Qiang has a history of thievery. He pilfers the belongings of a nun. He also absconds with silver candlestick holders and silver plates from a temple. Sam-seng-hiàn-gē

Sam-seng-hiàn-gē

Sam-seng-hiàn-gē presents a novel genre of music unique to Taiwan. The embraces the essence of local traditions, blending electronic music with elements of Taiwanese temple music, thus seamlessly crossing cultural boundaries. The exceptional band that was established by renowned music producer Blaire Ko. It also features two prominent figures in the electronic music scene, Sonic Deadhorse and fish.the, along with the talented young visual artist Children Chiu. Sam-seng-hiàn-gē channels the spirit of diverse expression and reverence for heaven and earth by embodying the essence of the three types of sacrifices: sea, land, and air. The band reflects on how to bridge the aesthetics passed down by their predecessors with contemporary expressions, encouraging more individuals to rediscover and embrace their cultural heritage. Through collaborations across different disciplines, they further offer fresh inspiration and new possibilities. Their aspiration is to serve as a conduit, linking digital sound with traditional culture, catering to music creators across all generations.

Go Go Machine Orchestra

Go Go Machine Orchestra

Established in 2017, the Go Go Machine Orchestra hails from Taiwan. Based in Kaohsiung, the ensemble crafts music using a diverse array of instruments, including piano, percussion, guitar, electronic noise, and synthesizers. The members of the orchestra are accomplished music and sound artists. The music style orbits around minimalism, intricately weaving together the arrangement, deconstruction, and reconstruction of diverse musical elements.

Presently, the ensemble has as members Kuan-Yin Chen on guitar, Ni-Li Tang on piano, Pin-Hsin Wang handling the synthesizer, and Ting-Chun Yeh on percussion. In 2019, they released their debut album Time, earning nominations at the 9th Golden Indie Music Awards for Best Live Performance, and at the 10th Golden Indie Music Awards for Best Crossover Music Album and Best Live Performance. That same year, they received an invitation to perform at the Asia Rolling Music Festival. Throughout their performances, they showcased a seamless fusion of image synthesis and minimalist rock music, epitomizing synergy. That year, their talents were sought after internationally, with invitations to perform in France, the Netherlands, and Poland. They made history as the first Taiwanese band to grace the stage at Les Trans Musicales de Rennes in France and secured another milestone as the premier Taiwanese band to be invited to record a live session for KEXP, a renowned American radio station. In both July 2020 and January 2021, they received invitations from the Kaohsiung Museum of Fine Arts to present Shift in Space as part of the museum's REOPEN series of activities. They participated in an online concert as part of the Primavera Pro music festival in Barcelona, Spain, in June 2021. In February 2022, they orchestrated the immersive live performance Illusion Fantasy featuring holographic projections. In October of the same year, they took part in the Baybeats music festival in Singapore. In May 2023, they received another invitation to the Wales Focus music festival in the UK, where their performance was warmly embraced.

As the repetitive machinery hums and noise builds, a formidable wall of sound emerges. Shapeless

DJ QuestionMark

“Shapeless” presents a performance through audiovisual means, interpreting the fluidity of forms through both auditory and visual elements. Many things are transient. Consciousness is transient, gender is transient, decisions are transient, and forms are also transient. From the perspective of quantum mechanics, all objects exist between tangible and intangible states, all being vibrations of quantum nature. The appearance of forms is merely determined by our subjective consciousness. Musically, “Shapeless” navigates through various genres like flowing water in a 40-minute composition. In terms of visual design, abundant abstract and shape-shifting elements echo the theme of Shapeless.

Formosa Follies

Nymphia Wind and the Haus of Wind

Nymphia Wind, renowned for introducing Taiwan to the world through her drag art, will lead the Haus of Wind girls Bagel Rimrim, Hannah Monina, Tina Banana, and Aphrodity to undergo a transformation into Taiwanese showgirls. They will take to an electronic float stage during the Cultural Olympics. Taiwan's island scenery and cultural landscape are translated into stunningly beautiful costumes. Witness how the Queen of the Haus of Wind showcases Taiwan's passion, diversity, faith, and myriad flavors through the adventurous aesthetics of cross-dressing. Let's craft a Taiwan impression with the audience in the birthplace of the Folies shows.

Crazy Girls

Eye Catching Circus

A captive soul, now gracefully morphing with the body’s fluidity, breaks free from constraints, riding on the surge of newfound liberty, emerging from the cocoon. Little island, big feast

Les Petites Choses Production

“Eating and drinking” is an indispensable part of life, and cuisine is the embodiment of the culture of each country/city. Taiwan and France each have their unique culinary traditions. A piece of music, a taste, can trigger personal emotions or shared memories. This performance will be inspired by a visible “call-in” to create, inviting local French dancers to collaborate. Let the ever-spinning Taiwanese lazy Susan round table lead everyone into the sensory memories of Taiwan.
