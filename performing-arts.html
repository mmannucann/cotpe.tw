Cultural Exhibition - Performing-Arts Series

Sound of Freedom

Capturing the essence of freedom, local sounds, and cross-cultural influences, in this event DJ Swallow pioneers a unique music style. The Hybrid Demi-Human group seamlessly incorporates local elements into their music. Meanwhile, the Pun Car Band, founded by Hsieh Ming-youu and known for passionately composing and performing songs celebrating their hometown, presents the charm and diversity of this Island of Freedom.

  • DJ Swallow

    DJ Swallow

    Party Queen DJ Swallow is a popular female performer from Taiwan. A prominent figure in show business, dance, and music, she is known for her appeal as a party queen. She's most comfortable and confident when she's on stage. The moment she comes out, excitement ripples through the crowd who soon turn into her devoted fans. In her performances she seamlessly blends various music genres, including EDM, Big Room, House, Trap, and Hardstyle. With an angelic face and a devilish physique, the audience loves both her appearance and her performance. Her remarkable success locally has led to invitations from abroad, where she has showcased her talents in various countries including Hong Kong, Macau, China, Singapore, Malaysia, Indonesia, and Australia. As an artist, dancer, and DJ, she effortlessly commands the atmosphere on stage. The audience closely follows her, immersing themselves in her unique music. Having been fond of dancing since childhood, rhythm comes naturally to her. DJ Swallow's motto: “Life requires music, and stage charm is essential!”

  • Hybrid Demi-Human

    Hybrid Demi-Human

    The Hybrid Demi-Human group rose to prominence following their participation in the Flowing Feast event in 2019, where they collaborated with Taiwan's LuxuryLogico mechanical teacher team on stage design, resulting in a groundbreaking success. Their music creation style is rooted in Techno, with sound concepts sampled from a shipyard in Kaohsiung. Sounds of metal work, along with elements of Taiwanese traditional opera and musical instruments are part of their music. Through a blend of DJ sampling, live musical instrument performances, and drumming, complemented by abstract VJ imagery steeped in Tai Chi and Taiwanese symbolism, the performances explore the dichotomy between darkness and light and the perpetual cycle of good and evil. They also offers a subversive reinterpretation of Taiwanese traditional art. Hybrid Demi-Human's creative focus lies in fusing Western electronic music with the rich tapestry of Taiwanese culture rooted in the East. This innovative performance style strives to communicate to the audience that, akin to the harmony found in Tai Chi where yin and yang interact seamlessly, the merging of Eastern and Western influences is also achievable.

  • Pun Car Band

    Pun Car Band

    Established 15 years ago, this band has serenaded audiences all over Taiwan, including performing in many temples. The band's core mission is to cater to the needs of the elderly. They frequently perform at nursing homes and kindergartens, regaling listeners with traditional or modified Taiwanese nursery rhymes. With over 2,000 performances under their belt, this band has dedicated themselves to give back to the older generation. Perhaps during your next visit to Tainan in southern Taiwan, you'll chance upon them. Harmonizing tales of Taiwan's history within the walls of a temple, their music extends warmth to every corner of the city.

Island Elegance

Presenting a fusion of contemporary and traditional elements, interweaving diverse artistic styles and cultural elements, this event showcases innovative works such as TAI Body Theatre's performances, DJ Dungi Sapor's electronic fusion, LIN Zong-fan/Feng-Chung-Teng spirit guiding group's traditional music, and DJ Elvis Lin’s modern tunes. Additionally, Chiu Shu-chan's Hakka fashion, ABAO's indigenous electronic mix, and the Questioning the Divine: Voices from the World new opera compilation project, as well as exquisite performances by the Taipei Puppet Theater all contribute to carrying the spirit of artistic evolution and cultural integration.

  • TAI Body Theatre

    TAI Body Theatre

    TAI Body Theatre was founded in 2013 by Truku choreographer Watan Tusi. In its early days, the dance company utilized an iron-clad hut situated under the Nongbing Bridge in Hualien City for rehearsals. It served as an experimental space to explore new body vocabulary inspired by indigenous cultures.

    “TAI”, originating from the Truku language, means “to see” or “to observe”. This selected term signifies Watan's gaze and contemplation of traditional indigenous culture, artistic director Watan pondered the following question: “In addition to traditional rituals or tourist performances, are there any new possibilities for indigenous music and dance?”

    Since 2013, Watan has found inspiration in the sound of trains as they traverse rails and the rhythmic movement of his feet. Gradually, he has discovered the profound connection between the movement of his feet and the earth beneath them. So far, he has meticulously documented over 80 styles of foot-scripts. Through each process of creative deconstruction and reorganization, novel bodily expressions and dance forms have emerged. The dance troupe employs traditional ritual music and dance as fundamental physical training for its members. Body movements then evolve from the TAI concept and its intricate foot-scripts. The performances by TAI Body Theatre encompass a wide range of styles. They delve into themes ranging from indigenous literature to the intertwining of body and music, as well as contemporary life and environmental conflicts within indigenous communities. The troupe has graced art festivals worldwide, including the Edinburgh Fringe Festival in the UK; the Jogja International Performing Arts Festival in Indonesia; the Wuzhen Theatre Festival in China; the Delhi International Arts Festival in India, and the YIRRAMBOI Festival in Australia. In 2016, the troupe received a nomination for the Taishin Arts Award for their performance Dancing Under the Bridge. They also won two first prizes at the Pulima Art Award for their productions “Terrace on the Hill” and “Weaving on the Moon”.

  • DUNGI SAPOR

    DUNGI SAPOR

    Dungi Sapor is a member of the Amis people, hailing from Hualien in eastern Taiwan. At the age of 18, she started her DJing journey in a college club, profoundly influenced by Enigma's Return to Innocence after encountering it during a course on intellectual property. This experience sparked her curiosity, leading her to ponder the question, “Why hasn't there been any integration between electronic and indigenous music?” She began to experiment with using electronic music as the backdrop and inviting tribal elders to sing and reinterpret traditional melodies. I Ho Yan became the first significant and successful single in her career. It was even used as halftime music during an NBA G League game. Dungi is dedicated to discovering avenues for bridging traditional and contemporary culture. Despite encountering unseen doubts, she never wavered in remembering her roots and identity. She has journeyed across the globe to both educate herself and promote her music. By engaging in numerous exchanges with diverse cultures, she fosters an international dialogue, hoping to facilitate a deeper integration of cultures across generations.

  • LIN Zong-fan/Feng-Chung-Teng spirit guiding group

    LIN Zong-fan/Feng-Chung-Teng spirit guiding group

    Lin Zong-fan, leader of the Feng-Chung-Teng Spirit Guiding Group, has dedicated many years to mastering the art of singing death songs. Through this journey, he has gradually accumulated cultural fragments and memories imbued with the beauty of death song singing. The music group was established in 2016. Feng-Chung-Teng (lit. “lantern in the wind”) symbolizes the lingering warmth of a departed individual. It also represents the guiding flame that draws the souls of the departed, led by the melody of the death song. The Feng-Chung-Teng Spirit Guiding Group preserves ancient and classical movements, as well as classic poetry. Its connotations, however, have been unified and modified by Lin Zong-fan. This is regarded as the most orthodox music group performing death songs in Taiwan.

  • DJ ELVIS LIN

    DJ ELVIS LIN

    DJ Elvis Lin has curated the music blog Music is the most beautiful thing in the world and has promoted independent music for nearly two decades. He started his career at the renowned Cao Chang bar in Taipei, performing shows such as Confession Night, Shoe Gazing Night, 90s Cover Night, and French Night. He has also initiated a soundtrack project, contributing to the re-scoring of films like Eternal Sunshine of the Spotless Mind and (500) Days of Summer. Invited to perform at various prestigious events, DJ Elvis Lin has shown his talents during the Taipei Film Festival's Toy Movie soundtrack, the Kaohsiung Film Festival's Back to the Future 35th Anniversary Re-release Party, the National Theater's TIFA Taiwan International Festival of Arts, as well as the Megaport Music Festival and the Simple Life Festival. Additionally, he has served as the opening DJ for notable acts such as the German electronic band Digitalism, the electronic band Fujiya & Miyagi, and electronic music artists Chad Valley, the last two from the UK.

  • Chiu Shu-chan

    Chiu Shu-chan

    Shu-Chan Chiu, a singer-songwriter hailing from Meinong and Liugui in Kaohsiung, has nurtured a lifelong affinity for her mother tongue, Hakka, since childhood. Securing the top honors in the Hakka category of both the 2020 and 2022 Taiwan Music Composition and Songwriting Contest, Shu-Chan Chiu triumphed with her singles Mong and Nightmare respectively. In July 2023, she unveiled her inaugural Hakka composition album, Shape Of Life. Completely independently produced and released, the album earned her the prestigious Best Folk Song Award at the 14th Golden Indie Music Awards. The album comprises soulful ballads about life, predominantly rooted in acoustic guitar melodies. Her mellifluous voice, akin to the first rays of dawn's warmth, intertwines with the morning dew, gently guiding the listener forward with tranquil assurance.

  • ABAO

    ABAO

    Abao (Aljenljeng)
    Singer, songwriter, and member of the Paiwan tribe in Taitung.

    In 2003, Abao made her debut with the girl group Abao & Brandy and secured the Golden Melody Award for Best Vocal Ensemble the subsequent year. Following her award win, Abao shifted her focus to music creation and immersed herself in behind-the-scenes production. In 2012, the producer of Taiwan Indigenous TV invited her to resume her role as a host on screen. As her hosting duties brought her closer to indigenous tribal cultures across Taiwan, she became increasingly involved in learning about and promoting indigenous cultures. While hosting, in 2014 she collaborated with her mother and grandmother to record the Paiwan folk ballad album “The East Payuan Folk And Three Generations”. In 2016, she released her first native-language album, “Vavayan Woman”. Her second native-language album, “Kinakaian Mother's Tongue”, released in 2019, garnered her eight Golden Melody Award nominations. Currently, she continues to intertwine mother tongue culture with multi-field performance work and advocates for the creation of mother tongue music by a new generation of indigenous youth.

  • Taipei Puppet Theater

    Taipei Puppet Theater

    Established in 2010, the Taipei Puppet Theater stands as Taiwan's first traditional puppet theater not operated as a typical family business. The theater's director, Lin Yong-chih, along with his team of young puppeteers, has sensed the rapid decline of Taiwan's traditional arts. Continuing the tradition has not been easy; however, the theater is dedicated to nurturing and perpetuating the exquisite old-time puppet show culture. The hope is that when the world sees Taiwan, it will also see the beauty and richness of puppet shows. The Taipei Puppet Theater has always maintained that for a stellar performance, both front-stage actors and back-stage musicians are indispensable. Years of rigorous training have elevated the theater to become the sole traditional theater in Taiwan boasting a fully equipped performance staff. For 13 consecutive years, the theater has been selected as Taiwan Top Performing Arts Group. Having performed over 600 times across the US, Europe, and Asia, the theater's shows have garnered widespread acclaim. Audiences have not only indulged in the audio-visual feast but have also received an introduction into Taiwan's rich and vibrant cultural heritage. The theater firmly believes that tradition never goes out of style; it simply needs to be appreciated and cherished. The allure of puppetry can endure indefinitely when infused with fresh ideas!

Contemporary New Art

Combining various forms of musical expression, this event features the group Sam-seng-hiàn-gē, which blends the essence of Taiwanese temples with electronic music, and 3PEOPLEMUSIC, who seamlessly integrate traditional Chinese instruments into modern melodies. Furthermore, A_Root mixes a variety of traditional and modern instruments, while the Go Go Machine Orchestra weaves piano, percussion, guitar, electronic noise, and synthesizers into minimalist compositions.

  • Sam-seng-hiàn-gē

    Sam-seng-hiàn-gē

    Sam-seng-hiàn-gē presents a novel genre of music unique to Taiwan. The embraces the essence of local traditions, blending electronic music with elements of Taiwanese temple music, thus seamlessly crossing cultural boundaries. The exceptional band that was established by renowned music producer Blaire Ko. It also features two prominent figures in the electronic music scene, Sonic Deadhorse and fish.the, along with the talented young visual artist Children Chiu. Sam-seng-hiàn-gē channels the spirit of diverse expression and reverence for heaven and earth by embodying the essence of the three types of sacrifices: sea, land, and air. The band reflects on how to bridge the aesthetics passed down by their predecessors with contemporary expressions, encouraging more individuals to rediscover and embrace their cultural heritage. Through collaborations across different disciplines, they further offer fresh inspiration and new possibilities. Their aspiration is to serve as a conduit, linking digital sound with traditional culture, catering to music creators across all generations.

  • 3PEOPLEMUSIC

    3PEOPLEMUSIC

    3 People Music has three young musicians as members, Kuo Jing-mu (zheng), PAN I-Tung (ruan), and JEN Chung (xiao). This is a rare creative chamber orchestra from Taiwan that innovates contemporary music while drawing from classical music as its foundation. In 2016, they were chosen for the Master Disciple and Partner Program at the National Center for Traditional Arts. In the same year, their self-titled album 3 People Music was released and received a nomination at the 28th Golden Melody Awards for Traditional Arts and Music. The second album, Change, released in 2021, won the Best Crossover Music Album Award at the 33rd Golden Melody Awards for Traditional Arts and Music. Moreover, it earned nominations for the Best Composition and Best Recording awards. In 2023, the digital album Catalysis: Fusion of Senses was released, earning a nomination for the Best Crossover Music Album Award at the 34th Golden Melody Awards for Traditional Arts and Music. It also secured a Bronze Award at the 2022 Global Music Awards. The trio is dedicated to traditional re-creation, cross-border collaboration, and contemporary music, actively participating in various forms of performances. They have already performed in China, Japan, South Korea, Singapore, India, Germany, France, and several other countries. Inviting musicians to perform together and commissioning composers to compose tailored music has also been a part of their approach, resulting in a more diverse and exciting soundscape.

  • Go Go Machine Orchestra

    Go Go Machine Orchestra

    Established in 2017, the Go Go Machine Orchestra hails from Taiwan. Based in Kaohsiung, the ensemble crafts music using a diverse array of instruments, including piano, percussion, guitar, electronic noise, and synthesizers. The members of the orchestra are accomplished music and sound artists. The music style orbits around minimalism, intricately weaving together the arrangement, deconstruction, and reconstruction of diverse musical elements.

    Presently, the ensemble has as members Kuan-Yin Chen on guitar, Ni-Li Tang on piano, Pin-Hsin Wang handling the synthesizer, and Ting-Chun Yeh on percussion. In 2019, they released their debut album Time, earning nominations at the 9th Golden Indie Music Awards for Best Live Performance, and at the 10th Golden Indie Music Awards for Best Crossover Music Album and Best Live Performance. That same year, they received an invitation to perform at the Asia Rolling Music Festival. Throughout their performances, they showcased a seamless fusion of image synthesis and minimalist rock music, epitomizing synergy. That year, their talents were sought after internationally, with invitations to perform in France, the Netherlands, and Poland. They made history as the first Taiwanese band to grace the stage at Les Trans Musicales de Rennes in France and secured another milestone as the premier Taiwanese band to be invited to record a live session for KEXP, a renowned American radio station. In both July 2020 and January 2021, they received invitations from the Kaohsiung Museum of Fine Arts to present Shift in Space as part of the museum's REOPEN series of activities. They participated in an online concert as part of the Primavera Pro music festival in Barcelona, Spain, in June 2021. In February 2022, they orchestrated the immersive live performance Illusion Fantasy featuring holographic projections. In October of the same year, they took part in the Baybeats music festival in Singapore. In May 2023, they received another invitation to the Wales Focus music festival in the UK, where their performance was warmly embraced.

    As the repetitive machinery hums and noise builds, a formidable wall of sound emerges. The electric guitar soars with high-pitched whines, while the xylophone delicately interweaves, rearranging and harmonizing the array of musical elements. Fragmented and reconstructed, minimalist melodies intertwine with digital compositions, slow rhythms, percussive beats, and electronic textures, forming a multifaceted tapestry of sound. It strikes the listener's senses like the stark contrast of a typical noodle shop tucked away in a roadside alley. In an era that champions interdisciplinary endeavors, the group actively seek collaborations with artists from diverse backgrounds and disciplines. It enables the audience to delve deeper into the poetry, accessing the soul through both visual and auditory pathways.

World Integration

  • Les Petites Choses Production

    Les Petites Choses Production

    From Art for People to Art with People.

    Les Petits Choses Production is a performing arts cooperative comprised of 17 creators. They are dedicated to reinventing and creating value in contemporary art and culture experience education. Through open participatory art practices, the troupe experiments within unconventional cultural and performance spaces. By collaborating with the public, they aim to establish more sustainable support networks for the arts. In 2020, Les Petits Choses Production was chosen as the representative team for the Edinburgh Fringe Festival: Taiwan Season by the Ministry of Culture and the Cultural Section of the Taipei Representative Office in the U.K. For three consecutive years, from 2021 to 2023, the troupe was honored as Taipei City Performing Arts Group of the Year, and in 2024 they were selected as Taiwan Top Performing Arts Group. In 2023, they received the Creative Awards by La Vie magazine.
