More than 4,000 spectators celebrate Taiwan Night in Paris

2024.7.28
URL address copied!

The day after the 2024 Olympic Games opening ceremony, which saw the French capital transformed into a veritable stage for the Cultural Olympiad on July 27, the Taiwan Pavilion was also inaugurated at Parc de la Villette. More than 4,000 spectators from all over the world gathered to enjoy a night full of Taiwanese flair in Paris. Vice Minister of Culture Sue Wang (王時思), Taiwanese Ambassador to France François Wu (Wu Chih-chung 吳志中), Director of the French Office in Taipei (BFT) Franck Paris, Honorary President of the World Taiwan Chambers of Commerce Tsai Kuo-Tai (蔡國泰), Honorary Presidents of the Taiwan Chambers of Commerce in Europe Jean-Charles Lam (林奇雋) and Sabine Chen (陳秫娟) attended the opening ceremony of the Taiwan Pavilion together.

The performances marking the first day of the Taiwan Pavilion began at 6:00 p.m. with DJ QuestionMark, who heated up the Taiwanese stage in an electro music party atmosphere. The performance was followed by the trio 3 Peoplemusic, who played rock and roll rhythms on traditional musical instruments, offering a new understanding of each of the traditional instruments. This was followed by Eye Catching Circus, Taiwan's first all-female circus, which moved the audience with its pure body language interpretation of the story of women able to rise up after being heavily affected by patriarchy. The cultural evening concluded with a flourish with the Golden Melody Award-winning singer ABAO, who broke through language barriers with her smooth voice and had the audience singing and dancing.

Vice Minister Sue Wang stated at the opening ceremony that on the Cultural Olympiad stage, Taiwan aims to showcase performances that transcend ethnicities, languages, genders, and time. In addition to allowing everyone to experience Taiwan's vivacity and innovation, the Taiwan Pavilion also aims to showcase its fearless cultural confidence to the world and demonstrate Taiwan's intention to contribute to the world. The Vice Minister also emphasized that no matter what situation Taiwan faces, the island will not retreat, concluding that Taiwan wants and needs to become a member of the world.

Taiwanese representative to France François Wu quoted Ernest Hemingway: "If you are lucky enough to have lived in Paris as a young man, wherever you go for the rest of your life, it never leaves you, because Paris is a party," to emphasize that the opening ceremony of the Paris Olympics showed the world the beauty of Paris and proved to the world the cultural greatness of France. The Cultural Olympiad that is about to begin will also demonstrate that, in addition to being a country of freedom, democracy, and possessing semiconductor know-how, Taiwan is also a country proud of its culture.

Franck Paris, the director of the BFT, was quick to point out that in addition to its bubble milk tea, Taiwan is also an ideal place to learn Mandarin, equipped with technological advances in semiconductors and artificial intelligence, and which shares the universal values of liberty, equality, and fraternity with other democratic countries.

The Ministry of Culture emphasized that from July 27 to August 10, Taiwan will meet the world with its culture and arts every evening at the Parc de la Villette in Paris. It also invites everyone to follow the performances of Taiwanese athletes at the Olympic Games and artists at the Cultural Olympiad through the ministry's official website and Facebook page .
